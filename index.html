Single & Multivariable

Calculus with Applications

in Neural Networks

Prof. Weiqing Gu
Fall 2020

Course Description

This course aims to bridge the gaps between various calculus techniques in academia and machine learning, such as neural networks, in the industrial world. Rigorous instruction in the theory, techniques, and applications of differential and integral calculus of a single variable together with a robust introduction to multivariable calculus and their connections to neural networks. Topics include limits, continuity, derivatives, definite integrals, infinite series, Taylor series in one and several variables, partial derivatives, double and triple integrals, linear approximations, the gradient, directional derivatives, and the Jacobian, optimization and the second derivative test, higher-order derivatives and Taylor approximations, line integrals, vector fields, curl, divergence, Green's theorem, and an introduction to flux and surface integrals. We will also introduce the math behind machine learning, such as backpropagation in neural networks.

You will learn how to read an academic paper with code samples from GitHub, give a summary of the paper, and reproduce the results using the provided code.

Upon completion of this course, we will provide a certificate of completion.

Summary of Goals

  • Gain a comprehensive view of the mathematics behind machine learning and neural networks.

  • Be able to search and read recent academic papers in the machine learning literature and apply those algorithms and concepts to real world problems.

  • Become comfortable with industry and academia standard tools (such as AWS and GitHub) and be able to find and work with large, public datasets.
